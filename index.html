GENboard is an engineered eco-board for interiors and furniture — a high-performance composite panel made from recycled textiles and designed as a low-carbon structural solution. GENboard converts mixed textile waste into structurally robust panels using water-based, biodegradable binders — designed for furniture, interiors and light construction, reducing dependence on conventional infrastructure boards.

Product overview

GENboard is a mixed-fabric composite panel engineered for performance (strength, fire resistance, moisture tolerance) while prioritising environmental safety and end-of-life options (compostable or safe recycling). Proprietary binder formulation is excluded from public materials.

Progress & milestones

Lab prototypes: Multiple lab-scale panels produced and tested (density, flexural strength, water absorption).
Material optimisation: Fiber blends and binder composition tuned to balance stiffness & toughness.
Pilot production: Bench press & pilot moulds used; finishing trials ongoing.
Standards & testing: Internal QA complete; next: third-party IS/ASTM-equivalent testing.
Finishing & Strength Validation: Industrial-standard finishing requires additional tooling — planned before commercial testing.
DFM & tooling: Production flow, tooling concept and BOM prepared for scale-up.

Key technical notes

  • Target density: 550–900 kg/m³ (adjustable by fiber mix).
  • Surface finishes: smooth, embossed, laminated options tested.
  • Binder: water-based biodegradable binder (confidential).
  • Standard sizes: 8ft × 4ft panels; variable thicknesses supported.
  • Industrial finishing tools planned before certification.

Compliance & Sustainability

GENboard is designed with sustainability across its entire lifecycle.

  • Raw Materials: Diverts textile waste from landfill, using eco-friendly binders.
  • Manufacturing: Low energy and water footprint; formaldehyde-free process.
  • Distribution: Regional supply chains to minimize emissions.
  • Use Phase: Durable, non-toxic, zero-VOC panels for healthier indoor air.
  • End-of-Life: Recyclable and biodegradable options ensure landfill diversion.
